Guest journey-state framing

Before the loyalty storage layer goes live, operators still need a shared way to describe where a guest sits in the continuity journey. The aim is not to over-model behavior, but to define a few practical journey states that support consistent decisions and prevent random outreach.

  • Freshly closed: the inquiry is complete, but it is too early to move into continuity action.
  • Warm monitor: the guest shows revisit potential and should remain visible for timing review.
  • Reactivation window: the best time for a soft continuity touch is likely approaching.
  • Referral-ready: the experience quality suggests advocacy or introduction potential.
  • Dormant archive: the record may still hold value later, but should not absorb active operator energy now.

A journey-state label should explain why a record is being watched, why it is being held back, or why it deserves a defined continuity lane without forcing the live Inquiry Queue to behave like a full CRM.
