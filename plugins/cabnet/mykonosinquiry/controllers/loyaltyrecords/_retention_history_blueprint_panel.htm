Retention history blueprint

The first activation wave should keep retention history narrow and useful. The goal is not a bloated CRM timeline, but a concise continuity record showing what was attempted, what happened, and what should happen next.

  • Keep the first history line focused on a small number of clearly defined touchpoint types.
  • Write summaries that explain outcome and next move, not free-form narrative sprawl.
  • Preserve enough evidence to support return-value handling, referral posture, and revisit timing.
  • Avoid mixing unresolved concierge execution work into the loyalty timeline.
