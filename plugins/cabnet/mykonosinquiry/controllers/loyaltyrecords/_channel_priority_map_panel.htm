Channel-priority map

Define the safest first outreach channel for each continuity lane before live storage activation. Treat the initial pilot as a channel-discipline exercise: only use routes that already fit the guest relationship, the service context, and the brand tone.

  • High-trust concierge lane: direct human follow-up for warm relationships with clear prior engagement.
  • Light-touch reminder lane: use low-pressure follow-up only when timing and prior service fit are strong.
  • Referral-ready lane: prioritize gratitude and introduction framing over promotional pressure.
  • Return-value lane: match the channel to likely response quality, not message volume.
  • Do-not-force rule: if the best channel is unclear, keep the record in Inquiry Queue or hold for later review.
