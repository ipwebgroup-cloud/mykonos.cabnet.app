Suppression controls

A live continuity pilot needs explicit stop-rules as much as it needs triggers. Define which cases must remain out of Loyalty Continuity even if a return-value signal exists, so operators do not overreach or collide with live service handling.

  • Service-friction suppression: unresolved dissatisfaction, open recovery work, or recent friction keeps the case out of continuity.
  • Active-handling suppression: any inquiry or guest still inside live operator handling stays in the Inquiry Queue.
  • Message-fatigue suppression: recent outbound contact without a fresh trigger pauses re-engagement.
  • Sensitivity suppression: premium, private, or delicate cases require senior review before any continuity move.
  • Ambiguity suppression: if the team cannot explain why continuity should happen now, hold the case back.
