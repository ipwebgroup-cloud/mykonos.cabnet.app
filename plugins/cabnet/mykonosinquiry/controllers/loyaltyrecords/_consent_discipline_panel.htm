Consent discipline

Loyalty Continuity should stay premium and trustworthy. Before any pilot outreach is activated, the team should define which outreach assumptions are safe, which require clearer prior relationship context, and which should remain out of scope until the continuity storage layer is deliberately enabled.

  • Relationship-first rule: continuity belongs to known, completed, or clearly concluded journeys, not ambiguous inbound leads.
  • Channel-confidence rule: only use channels where the team can explain why the guest would reasonably expect a continuity touch.
  • Recent-service rule: the closer the prior service relationship, the lower the ambiguity for a gentle return-value contact.
  • Luxury-tone rule: continuity should feel bespoke and respectful, never like broad campaign automation.
  • Hold-when-unclear rule: when the team cannot defend the appropriateness of contact, the case stays out of loyalty activation.
