Return-value signals

The safest first loyalty activation should focus on return-value signals that are already visible from inquiry and concierge handling, without inventing a separate guest-memory system too early.

  • Strong close quality: the original engagement ended clearly rather than drifting unresolved.
  • Meaningful service fit: the guest used or requested services that naturally support a future continuity offer.
  • Repeat-friendly timing: the stay pattern suggests a believable return window.
  • Referral potential: the record shows brand trust or concierge satisfaction that could justify outreach.
  • Low operational risk: re-engagement can happen without confusing the active Inquiry Queue.

These signals help define a narrow first cohort for loyalty activation while keeping VIP memory, repeat-guest intelligence, and active triage clearly separated.
