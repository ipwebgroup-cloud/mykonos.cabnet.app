Mitigation playbook

This playbook translates the main launch risks into practical safeguards. The goal is to make continuity planning feel disciplined and reversible before any structural storage release is considered.

  • Mitigate qualification risk: require a documented reason the guest belongs in continuity and not in the active inquiry queue.
  • Mitigate treatment risk: match each pilot lane to one clearly defined message family and one channel priority order.
  • Mitigate volume risk: apply suppression, cap, and consent checkpoints before any outbound continuity action is approved.
  • Mitigate control risk: keep rollback criteria, approval checkpoints, and post-launch review guardrails visible in the same workspace.
  • Mitigate scaling risk: expand only after pilot measurement, return-value signals, and the launch decision register indicate stable results.
