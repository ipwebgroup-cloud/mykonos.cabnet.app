Touchpoint outcome taxonomy

Once the touchpoint ledger is activated, every retention contact should resolve into a small and reusable outcome set. This keeps Loyalty Continuity useful for return-value handling instead of collapsing into vague notes.

  • Revisit interest: the guest signals credible timing for another stay or service cycle
  • Referral posture: the record shows social proof or introduction potential worth tracking
  • Nurture hold: goodwill remains, but timing is premature for stronger activation
  • No-response / soft decline: contact was attempted but should not trigger repetitive follow-up
  • Do not continue: the retention lane should suppress future continuity effort unless context changes
