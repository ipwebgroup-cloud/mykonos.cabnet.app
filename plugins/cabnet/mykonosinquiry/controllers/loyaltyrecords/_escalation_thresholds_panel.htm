Escalation thresholds

The first continuity pilot should define clear thresholds for when a case stops being a normal retention candidate and must be elevated back into premium operator handling. This keeps Loyalty Continuity supportive, not reckless.

  • Relationship sensitivity: if the guest relationship has unusual complexity, keep a senior operator in the loop.
  • Commercial sensitivity: high-value or strategically important cases should have explicit escalation ownership.
  • Recovery sensitivity: unresolved friction, disappointment, or ambiguity should route out of continuity planning.
  • Timing sensitivity: when the ideal re-engagement window is uncertain, pause rather than force outreach.
  • Brand sensitivity: if the message could feel intrusive or off-tone, escalate before sending anything live.
