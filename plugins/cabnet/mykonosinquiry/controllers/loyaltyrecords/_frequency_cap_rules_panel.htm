Frequency-cap rules

The first live continuity wave should run with conservative pacing. Frequency caps protect the premium brand experience by preventing too many touches across overlapping channels or short windows.

  • One-intent rule: one meaningful re-engagement goal per cycle, not multiple competing asks.
  • Channel spacing: avoid stacking email, WhatsApp, phone, and concierge follow-up too tightly together.
  • Cooling period: after a continuity touch, allow time for response or silence before the next step.
  • Escalation override: only exceed standard cadence when an approved trigger and ownership path justify it.
  • Pilot discipline: start with lower-volume pacing until the team has real evidence from the first wave.
